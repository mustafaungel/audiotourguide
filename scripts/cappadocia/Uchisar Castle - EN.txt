Hello everyone! Welcome to the crown of Cappadocia - Uçhisar Castle.
Today, I will lead you through time and space to explore the
thousand-year-old story of this extraordinary fortress.

The Origin and Strategic Position of the Castle

Please look at the huge rock in front of us. It is not an ordinary
mountain peak but the highest point in the Cappadocia region. In ancient
times, the early inhabitants recognized the unique geographical
advantages of this place and carved out the initial caves in the soft
volcanic rock for protection and shelter. It is not hard to imagine that
in the era of cold weapons, occupying such a commanding height meant
controlling the surrounding valleys and transportation routes.

Historically, Uçhisar Castle has always been the main defense point of
the entire Cappadocia region. Its strategic value was recognized and
utilized by multiple civilizations, from the Byzantine period to the
later Ottoman Empire, all of which fortified it. Notably, during the
Persian and Seljuk periods, it even became the main control center for
important trade routes such as the "Royal Road" and the "Silk Road". It
can be said that this castle has witnessed the prosperity and changes of
the entire region.

The ingenious architectural wisdom

Please take a close look at the surface of the castle, those densely
packed holes. They are not formed by natural weathering but are
carefully designed pigeon houses by the ancients. Don't underestimate
these pigeons; they were not only a source of food, but their droppings
were also precious fertilizer for agricultural cultivation at that time.
The interior of the castle is a complex structure like a maze, with
tunnels, rooms and a network of passages. At its peak, it was equipped
with complete living facilities such as living rooms and storage rooms.
When threatened, residents could retreat inside and live for a long time
relying on the stored supplies.

Please be careful when climbing as these steps are indeed quite steep.
However, it was precisely through such a design that was easy to defend
and difficult to attack that our ancestors were able to resist the
invasion of foreign enemies. In the 15th and 16th centuries, this castle
was used as an important defense mechanism.

With the passage of time, the military function of the castle gradually
faded away. It was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1985
and became part of a national park in 1986, officially becoming a
cultural treasure jointly protected by all mankind.

Now, standing here, although we no longer see the figures of soldiers on
patrol as in the past, we can enjoy a 360-degree panoramic view that
keeps your camera clicking non-stop. On days with good visibility, you
can clearly see the map of Cappadocia - the valleys, villages, and
castles. Looking far into the distance, you can even see the snow on the
top of Mount Erciyes, outlined by the wind and the sky. Standing here,
you will understand why Uçhisar is called the crown of Cappadocia.
